# Theory: Aerodynamics

Symbols and Julia identifiers follow the [canonical notation](@ref notation).

## Assumptions
- The aircraft is rigid. Therefore, the aerodynamic force and moment do not depend on aircraft configuration states.
- Flow surrounding the aircraft is uniform, and the aircraft does not disturb the flow in which is immersed. Therefore, the air-relative velocity $\bm{v}_r$ and apparent air-relative angular velocity $\bm{\omega}_r$ are the only flow states upon which the aerodynamic model depends.
- All aerodynamic memory is represented by a finite-dimensional internal state, $\bm{x}_\mathrm{aero}$. Effects involving actuator rates $\dot{\bm{\delta}}$, aerodynamic angle rates such as $\dot{\alpha}$, or general unsteady effects are captured by the time evolution of $\bm{x}_\mathrm{aero}$.

## Inputs and Outputs

**Inputs:**
- Air-relative velocity $\bm{v}_r \in \mathbb{R}^3$
- Apparent air-relative angular velocity $\bm{\omega}_r \in \mathbb{R}^3$
- Actuator state $\bm{\delta} \in \mathbb{R}^{p_\mathrm{act}}$
- Unsteady model state $\bm{x}_\mathrm{aero} \in \mathbb{R}^{n_\mathrm{aero}}$
- Stochastic residual model state $\bm{x}_\mathrm{res} \in \mathbb{R}^{n_\mathrm{res}}$

**Outputs:**
- Aerodynamic force $\bm{F} \in \mathbb{R}^3$ and moment $\bm{M} \in \mathbb{R}^3$
- Unsteady model state time derivative $\dot{\bm{x}}_\mathrm{aero} \in \mathbb{R}^{n_\mathrm{aero}}$
- Stochastic residual model state time derivative $\dot{\bm{x}}_\mathrm{res} \in \mathbb{R}^{n_\mathrm{res}}$

## Aerodynamic Model
The nominal model for the aerodynamic force and moment is assumed to have the followign functional dependence:
```math
	\begin{gathered}
		\bm{F}_\mathrm{model}(\bm{v}_r,\bm{\omega}_r,\bm{x}_\mathrm{aero},\bm{\delta}) \\ 
		\bm{M}_\mathrm{model}(\bm{v}_r,\bm{\omega}_r,\bm{x}_\mathrm{aero},\bm{\delta})
	\end{gathered}
```

## Unsteady Aerodynamics
The internal state of the unsteady aerodynamic model satisfies
```math
	\dot{\bm{x}}_\mathrm{aero} = \bm{f}_\mathrm{aero}(\bm{x}_\mathrm{aero},\bm{v}_r,\bm{\omega}_r,\bm{\delta})
```
The state may represent latent approximations of unsteady aerodynamics, dynamic stall, wake states, or causal approximations of derivative-dependent quasi-steady terms. A quasi-steady aerodynamic model has $n_\mathrm{aero} = 0$.

### Stochastic Residual Model (Aerodynamic Modeling Error)
Aerodynamic modeling error is represented as the diffusion process $\bm{x}_\mathrm{res} \in \mathbb{R}^{n_\mathrm{res}}$, which is generated by the SDE
```math
	\mathrm{d}\bm{x}_\mathrm{res} = \bm{f}_\mathrm{res}(\bm{x}_\mathrm{res},\bm{v}_r,\bm{\omega}_r,\bm{x}_\mathrm{aero},\bm{\delta}) \mathrm{d}t + \bm{\sigma}_\mathrm{res}(\bm{x}_\mathrm{res},\bm{v}_r,\bm{\omega}_r,\bm{x}_\mathrm{aero},\bm{\delta}) \mathrm{d} \bm{W}_\mathrm{res}
```
where $\bm{W}_\mathrm{res}$ is a Wiener process defined on $\mathbb{R}^{m_\mathrm{res}}$. The perturbation to the force and moment model, that is the model residual, is
```math
	\begin{bmatrix} 
		\delta\bm{F} \\ 
		\delta\bm{M} 
	\end{bmatrix} = \bm{g}_\mathrm{res}(\bm{x}_\mathrm{res},\bm{v}_r,\bm{\omega}_r,\bm{x}_\mathrm{aero},\bm{\delta}) \bm{x}_\mathrm{res}
```

## Simulated Force and Moment
Altogether, the simulated force and moment are
```math
	\begin{aligned}
		\bm{F} &= \bm{F}_\mathrm{model}(\bm{v}_r,\bm{\omega}_r,\bm{x}_\mathrm{aero},\bm{\delta}) + \delta\bm{F} \\
		\bm{M} &= \bm{M}_\mathrm{model}(\bm{v}_r,\bm{\omega}_r,\bm{x}_\mathrm{aero},\bm{\delta}) + \delta\bm{M}
	\end{aligned}
```

## Fixed-Wing Aerodynamics

Let the air-relative velocity comprise the components $\bm{v}_r = [u_r~v_r~w_r]^\mathsf{T}$. Then, the airspeed $V$, angle of attack $\alpha$, and sideslip angle $\beta$ are
```math
	\begin{gathered}
		V = \|\bm{v}_r\| \\
		\alpha = \mathrm{tan}_4^{-1}(w_r,u_r) \\
		\beta = \sin^{-1}(v_r/V)
	\end{gathered}
```
where $\mathrm{tan}_4^{-1}$ is the four-quadrant arctangent function.
